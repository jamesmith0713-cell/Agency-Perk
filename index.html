The old way

Spiffs are a great idea that usually turns into a mess

If your spiff program runs on a shared spreadsheet, sticky notes, or "just remember what you sold," you've probably run into all three of these.

✕

Nobody agrees on the numbers

By Friday, three people have three different counts for the week — and someone's payout is wrong.

✕

Doubling math gets done by hand

Whoever runs payroll has to remember which sales crossed the threshold and recalculate the rate — every single week.

✕

Verification is "trust me"

Without a check against actual policy records, spiffs get paid on sales that didn't stick — or disputed sales never get resolved.

How it works

Four steps, every week, on repeat

The same flow runs Monday through Sunday for every rep on your team — no setup required after the first time.

01
Log

A rep makes a sale and logs it on the spot

They pick their name, tap in their spiff counts (PIF, bundles, whatever your agency tracks), and enter a reference — a policy number, a row number from your tracking sheet, whatever makes sense for your office. For bundles spanning two policies, both references are captured.

02
Verify

Your verifier checks it against the real record

A simple three-point check — profile matches, auto-pay is active, the policy is still active — plus their initials. Nothing gets marked approved until someone's actually looked at it.

03
Track

The leaderboard updates itself, including doubling

Cross your agency's threshold on a spiff type in a week, and the remaining units automatically pay double — no one has to remember to calculate it. Everyone can see where they stand, all week.

04
Pay

Friday, export and hand it to payroll

One click gives you a clean CSV of approved payouts, broken down by rep and spiff type — ready for whoever cuts the checks. No re-typing, no recalculating.
